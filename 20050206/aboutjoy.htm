ABOUT JOY AND ABOUT SWALLOWHILL

Joy Parker lives in Midland, Ontario, Canada - Midland is a small town on the shores of Georgian Bay - part of the Great Lakes system. Joy began making miniature porcelain dolls in 1974 and began selling them in 1975 - well you can only make so many dolls for your collection and to give to friends and relatives, so it was either do something else or start offering the kits for sale.

Since the name of the farm where Joy, husband Wayne and three boys used to live. was "Swallowhill", and since that name was already registered, (for purebred milking goats!) that seemed the name to use for the doll business.

About 1978 Joy met Sue Atkinson, of London, England and started a long lasting friendship. Sue started dressing the doll kits and after a couple of years, she agreed that she would like to be the British and European distributor for Joy's kits.

Sue, who wrote the highly acclaimed book "Making and Dressing Dollshouse Dolls in 1/12th Scale", published by David & Charles in England, called her business "Sunday Dolls", and sold her beautifully dressed dolls all over the world.

For a lot of years Joy and Wayne worked together with two employees and sold thousands of doll kits across the world, but these days Joy works mostly on her own - semi-retirement is wonderful. Son John keeps her company during the day, with Joy working on the doll kits and John working on his original molds for miniatures, and his porcelain ceramic blanks. John's two children come with him, which is wonderful but a mixed blessing at times *grin*.

Joy is known for her delicate china painting, and for her dolls dressed in the English manner - sew the costumes and use glue only where absolutely necessary.

This is a photo of John and Joy, taken at the 2001 WIMMS show by Frances Armstrong. You can see that John and Joy are good friends, which is handy when you work together as much as they do *grin*.
